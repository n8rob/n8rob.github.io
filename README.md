Hi, I'm Nate. I am a third-year PhD student at Johns Hopkins University’s [Center for Language and Speech Processing](https://www.clsp.jhu.edu/) (as of spring 2026, when I'm writing this). I am advised by [Kenton Murray](https://kentonmurray.com/) and [Sanjeev Khudanpur](https://engineering.jhu.edu/faculty/sanjeev-khudanpur/). In 2023 I completed my [Masters in Language Technologies](https://www.lti.cs.cmu.edu/academics/masters-programs/mlt.html) at Carnegie Mellon University under the advisement of [David Mortensen](https://www.cs.cmu.edu/~dmortens/), and I researched at Brigham Young University’s [DRAGN Labs](https://dragn.ai/) under [Nancy Fulda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nancy_Fulda) prior to that. 

My research focuses on building language technologies, including machine translation and speech technologies, for low-resource and related languages. I speak English, French, Spanish, Arabic, and Haitian proficiently through years of study. In part due to this background, I am especially interested in building technologies for groups of related language varieties such as Creole languages and dialectal Arabic varieties. I am likewise interested in Romance, Bantu, Indo-Aryan, Dravidian, and Sinitic languages, *inter alia*. 

I am a nerd about linguistics and languages, as well as math, books, music, natural sciences, and world cultures.
